Lasagna

Lasagna is an Italian dish made with layers of pasta, meat, and cheese, and baked in the oven until hot and bubbly.

To make lasagna, you will need to start by preheating your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Next, you will need to cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, and then set them aside. In a separate pan, you will need to brown some ground beef or sausage, and then drain off the excess fat. Next, you will need to prepare a simple tomato sauce by simmering crushed tomatoes, garlic, and Italian seasoning in a pan. Once everything is prepared, you can start assembling the lasagna by layering the noodles, meat, and sauce in a 9x13 inch baking dish. Repeat this process until you have used up all of your ingredients, making sure to end with a layer of sauce on top. Cover the dish with foil and bake it in the oven for about 45 minutes, or until the lasagna is heated through and the cheese on top is melted and bubbly. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Ingredients

  • Italian Sausage
  • Lean Ground Beef
  • 1 Onion
  • 2 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Can of Crushed Tomatoes
  • 2 Cans of Tomato Sauce
  • 2 Cans of Tomato Paste
  • 2 Tablespoons of White Sugar
  • Fresh Parsley
  • Dried Basil Leaves
  • Salt
  • Italian Seasoning
  • Fennel Seeds
  • Black Pepper
  • Lasagna Noodles
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Mozzarella Cheese
  • Ricotta Cheese
  • An Egg

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
